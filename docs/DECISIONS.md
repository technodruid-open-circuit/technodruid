## 2026-05-16 — DECISION-FONTS — Self-host Fira Mono
**Chosen:** Self-host Fira Mono weights 400, 500, 700 in `public/fonts/`. Remove the existing Google Fonts `<link>` from `src/layouts/Base.astro`.
**Alternatives:** Loading from `fonts.googleapis.com`; switch to system monospace stack; introduce a second typeface for canonical content.
**Why:** User decision 2026-05-16. INV-DOC-1 forbids third-party font hosts (privacy-of-the-keeper doctrine, IA §1). Fira Mono is already in use; self-hosting preserves the existing aesthetic without doctrine slip. Adding a second typeface for canonical content was rejected to avoid drift from the CRT/terminal register.
**Trade-offs:** Repository carries ~150 KB of font files. Updates require manual download rather than automatic CDN refresh.
**Revisit if:** A user-tested redesign requires book-typography for canonical content that monospace cannot serve.

## 2026-05-16 — DECISION-FORK-POLICY — Forks encouraged
**Chosen:** Forks are explicitly encouraged and stated as such on `/about/community`.

**Why:** IA §9 Q7. The Open Circuit doctrine treats forks as healthy divergence, not abandonment. Encouraging them publicly signals the doctrine.

## 2026-05-20 — DECISION-MONIKER — Maintainer name is the moniker held in the Circuit, taken in commitment
**Chosen:** The maintainer's public name on the site is the name they hold within the Circuit — taken on entering the practice, in commitment to the core tenets. It may be the practitioner's given name or a name chosen at the threshold; both are sincere. Transparency is carried by the commitment, not by the form of the name. The Rite §VIII ("The Naming") is the canonical source; this DECISION aligns the legal-framing surfaces (`/about/origins`, `/about/contact`) with what the Rite already says and with what the site already does — the initial maintainer is listed as **Shakes**, not by the maintainer's given name.
**Alternatives:** (A) Require legal/given names on the legal-framing surface (the original DECISION-MAINTAINER rationale, 2026-05-16). (B) Permit fully anonymous maintainership (no public-facing name at all). (C) Require both — a moniker *and* a given name listed alongside it.
**Why:** The Rite §VIII permits "given name, or a chosen practitioner name." Reframing transparency around the commitment-marker (Buddhist Dharma-name / monastic-naming pattern, where adopting a new name publicly marks vocational commitment) resolves the contradiction without weakening accountability — the moniker maps to a single PR-signing identity in commit history, so the maintainer is no less reachable. Option B (full anonymity) was rejected: it severs the accountability chain entirely. Option C was rejected: it imposes a forced disclosure that the doctrine does not require and contradicts the Rite's "may be the given name *or* a chosen practitioner name" framing.
**Trade-offs:** "Real name on the legal-framing surface" was a strong skeptic-facing signal (IA §3 persona "The Skeptical"). The new framing requires the skeptic to read one paragraph further on `/about/origins` and `/about/contact` to find the commitment claim. Mitigated by: maintainer accountability remains public via PR-signing identity; change process and contact channels are unchanged; the new framing is doctrinally derived from the Rite rather than invented for this DECISION. A reader who insists on seeing a legal-name signal will not find one; this is a deliberate trade-off rather than an accidental gap.
**Revisit if:** A legal or regulatory context (trademark holder, registered nonprofit, court filing) requires a legal-name disclosure on a specific surface; OR the initial maintainer chooses to publish their given name alongside the moniker; OR a maintainer's moniker is shown to mislead in a way that undermines accountability (e.g. impersonation, namespace collision with another practitioner).

## 2026-05-25 — DECISION-INV-A11Y-TIMEOUT — INV-A11Y-1 deferred from Phase 3 SG-11 onward, re-arms on SG-15
**Chosen:** INV-A11Y-1 (zero axe-core serious/critical violations on every route) is recorded as PASS-CARRYOVER for Phase 3 SG-11 onward and excluded from each iteration's pre-flight enforcement until SG-15 lands. SG-15's scope (`tests/a11y-full.spec.ts`) is amended to restructure the suite as one Playwright test per route — each route gets its own 30s budget rather than sharing one aggregate timeout. Static evidence (axe results from a manual `pnpm preview` + per-page run) may still be requested ad-hoc; what is deferred is the suite-level assertion's reliability, not the underlying accessibility requirement.
**Alternatives:** (A) Add `playwright.config.ts` with a longer global test timeout (60s or 120s) — buys headroom for the existing one-big-test structure but does not address the architectural fragility; each new Phase-3 route still pushes the cumulative time up. (B) Refactor `tests/a11y.spec.ts` in this iteration (out of SG-11 scope) — fixes the issue immediately but bundles unrelated work into a trivial-tier content iteration. (C) Treat the timeout as a hard FAIL and halt SG-11 — would gate /about hub-page work behind unrelated test-infrastructure work.
**Why:** User decision 2026-05-25. The failure root cause is structural: `tests/a11y.spec.ts:33-52` runs all dist routes in a single Playwright test with `await test.step(...)` per route; axe-core analysis takes ~1.5s per route, and at 24 routes the cumulative cost crossed the default 30s per-test timeout between iter 10 (where it passed cleanly) and iter 11 (where two consecutive runs failed on different /library/* routes). The failure is not caused by any SG-11 edit (which only touched `/about/index.astro`); the build and astro check are clean. SG-15's planned `tests/a11y-full.spec.ts` is the canonical place to address this: it already needs to codify the full route inventory and assert zero violations, and restructuring it as one-test-per-route gives each route an independent 30s budget that does not erode as content grows. Adding `playwright.config.ts` now (option A) would leave the architectural problem in place; refactoring in SG-11 (option B) violates "one iteration unit per iteration." This decision joins DECISION-INV-NAV-1-DEFER and DECISION-INV-NAV-2-DEFER as the third documented deferral in Phase 3, which itself triggers a process check — see Revisit if.
**Trade-offs:** INV-A11Y-1's suite-level mechanical verification is offline for up to 4 iterations (SG-11 through SG-14) before SG-15 re-arms it. Mitigation: (1) the underlying accessibility requirement is unchanged — pages must still pass axe-core; (2) the failure mode is a timeout (no false negatives masking real violations — when the test completes, its result is correct); (3) ad-hoc per-page axe checks remain available for any iteration that introduces a route with unusual a11y risk; (4) SG-15 is a Phase-3 ISC, not a stretch goal, so the deferral window is bounded. The DECISION-INV-NAV-2-DEFER `Revisit if` clause flagged "a third unrelated invariant proposed for deferral" as a process-failure trigger. Acknowledged here: three deferrals in one phase is the upper bound of what the framework should absorb. If a fourth invariant deferral surfaces before Phase 3 closes, the loop's pre-flight model itself needs revisiting (e.g., a `flaky_threshold` concept for time-bounded suites distinct from doctrine deferrals).
**Revisit if:** SG-15 lands and INV-A11Y-1 still fails or remains flaky after the per-route restructure; OR a fourth unrelated invariant is proposed for deferral within Phase 3 (treat as a process check, not another DECISION); OR an iteration introduces a route with elevated a11y risk (form-heavy, complex aria) where suite-level deferral is insufficient and ad-hoc verification is required as part of the iteration's VERIFY.

## 2026-05-25 — DECISION-I18N-PATTERN — Translation storage uses sibling `src/content/translations/<lang>/<doc-id>.md` collection (not per-collection lang subdirs)
**Chosen:** Translations of canonical and living documents live in `src/content/translations/<lang>/<doc-id>.md`, structured as a sibling collection to `canonical/` and `living/`. The canonical English source remains the authoritative entry in its native collection (e.g., `src/content/canonical/codex.md`); a Spanish translation of the Codex would live at `src/content/translations/es/codex.md`. The `<lang>` segment uses the BCP 47 language subtag (e.g., `es`, `de`, `fr`, `zh-Hans`). The `<doc-id>` matches the source entry's id within its native collection. No `translations` collection schema is registered in `src/content.config.ts` until the first translation lands — Phase 3's infrastructure ISC requires only the directory and the documented pattern, not a populated collection.
**Alternatives:** (A) **Astro's per-collection lang convention** — translations would live at `src/content/canonical/<lang>/<doc-id>.md` (e.g., `canonical/es/codex.md`), co-located with the source. This is the pattern Astro's content i18n examples encourage and the routing path of least resistance. (B) **URL-prefix routing without separate content files** — store translations as frontmatter variants on the source entry. Rejected outright: doesn't scale, conflates source-of-truth, breaks version history per-translation. (C) **Per-doc-id directories with locale frontmatter** — `src/content/canonical/codex/{en.md,es.md,...}`. Reorganises the existing canonical structure, which is doctrinally costly.
**Why:** The CC BY-SA 4.0 license treats a translation as a derivative work. The canonical English Codex is the *authoritative* doctrine; a Spanish translation is a *service to* the canonical, not a parallel canonical. Co-locating both in the same collection (option A) would blur this boundary — the file tree would suggest translations are peer entries when they are derivative. A sibling `translations/` directory keeps the canonical-as-doctrine vs. translation-as-derivative distinction visible in the file system, matching the doctrinal commitment from IA §3 ("Canonical — versioned, stable, slow-changing... Living — wiki-style, fast-changing"). The PRD's Key Files table already specifies `src/content/translations/<lang>/` as the intended structure (Phase 3 PRD line 53), so this DECISION ratifies the PRD-stated intent. ISC-P3-I18N-2 explicitly permits a documented pattern as an alternative to Astro's default convention, making this departure sanctioned and just needing the documentation this entry provides.
**Trade-offs:** Astro's automatic i18n routing is optimized for option A; with this pattern, routing translated pages will require either: (a) a `[lang]/[...slug].astro` dynamic route iterating `getCollection("translations")`, or (b) per-translation Astro pages stamped manually. Either approach is straightforward but is one extra integration step Phase 4 will own. Future tooling that wants per-collection translation coverage (e.g., "how complete is the Spanish set?") must walk two directory trees rather than one. The trade-off is acceptable because: (1) ISC-P3-I18N-2 only requires *infrastructure*, not working translation routing; (2) the doctrinal clarity outweighs the routing-convenience cost; (3) Phase 4's "first translation" iteration will be the natural moment to register a `translations` collection schema with required fields (`sourceCollection`, `sourceId`, `locale`, `translator`, `translatedAt`, etc.) — premature schema design now is exactly the pattern CLAUDE.md "don't design for hypothetical future requirements" forbids.
**Revisit if:** The first translation lands (Phase 4) and the sibling-directory pattern proves friction-y for routing (re-evaluate option A then); OR Astro releases native i18n routing that explicitly supports sibling collections (lower migration cost); OR a tooling or analytics requirement makes per-collection co-location materially preferable.

## 2026-05-26 — DECISION-PLAUSIBLE-KEEP — Plausible Cloud is the permanent analytics choice (DECISION-PLAUSIBLE resolved)
**Chosen:** Plausible Cloud (`plausible.io`) remains in `Plausible.astro`; no self-host migration. The 2026-05-16 DECISION-PLAUSIBLE's "temporary doctrine slip" framing is retired — the exception is now permanent and tied to this DECISION. `docs/invariant_requirements.md` INV-DOC-2 is amended in the same change to drop "temporarily until DECISION-PLAUSIBLE resolves" and key the exception to this entry.
**Alternatives:** (A) Self-host Plausible — incurs server/TLS/update operational burden for a vendor that already meets the privacy-of-the-keeper standard at source. (B) Remove analytics entirely — loses the only signal of doctrine propagation. (C) Switch to self-hosted GoatCounter or umami — same operational cost as (A) for no doctrinal gain over Plausible Cloud's already-privacy-preserving model.
**Why:** Plausible Hosting BV's business model is direct payment by site owners, not surveillance monetization. The product itself sets no cookies, performs no cross-site tracking, collects no personal data, hosts EU-side, and is GDPR-compliant out of the box. The 2026-05-16 framing assumed self-hosting was the doctrinally pure endpoint; on reflection, "privacy of the keeper" concerns what the reader experiences, not server location. A privacy-aligned vendor whose incentives align with reader privacy is functionally equivalent to self-hosting from the reader's perspective. INV-DOC-2 remains a single-vendor exception — no slippery slope.
**Trade-offs:** A third-party domain still loads on every page. Vendor-risk is the residual concern: acquisition or business-model pivot would convert this back into a real doctrine slip overnight. The Revisit clause is the protection.
**Revisit if:** Plausible Hosting BV is acquired, pivots its business model, introduces cookies or cross-site tracking, or otherwise materially diverges from current privacy commitments; OR a maintained self-host distribution becomes operationally trivial (managed self-host, container-only deploy with zero TLS plumbing); OR reader complaints surface around even this minimal third-party load.

## 2026-05-26 — DECISION-REPO-KEEP — Stay on GitHub for canonical source hosting (DECISION-REPO resolved)
**Chosen:** The canonical repository remains at `https://github.com/technodruid-open-circuit/technodruid`. No Forgejo migration. `src/consts.ts` `SOURCE_REPO_URL` is unchanged. The Phase 4 ISC-P4-REPO-1 "either Forgejo or documented stay-on-GitHub" requirement is satisfied by the latter.
**Alternatives:** (A) Migrate to a self-hosted Forgejo instance — eliminates Microsoft/GitHub from the supply chain; adds hosting/upgrade/backup operational burden. (B) Migrate to Codeberg (Forgejo-as-a-service) — sheds Microsoft without operational cost but trades platform-risk dependency rather than removing it. (C) Mirror passively to a self-hosted Forgejo while keeping GitHub as canonical — doubles operational surface for partial doctrinal gain.
**Why:** The site's content and code are entirely public and CC BY-SA / open-source. GitHub's surveillance posture is irrelevant to a fully-public project — there is no private data for Microsoft to monetize, and Microsoft's hosting of public Git history confers discoverability benefits (search, contributor onboarding, GitHub Actions ecosystem, third-party tooling) that align with the Open Circuit doctrine of propagation. The Forgejo-migration concern in the implicit DECISION-REPO presupposed that a privacy-aligned project must avoid surveillance-aligned hosts; this conflates user-data privacy with vendor-association cleanliness. The doctrine is about what readers and contributors experience — both are unchanged on GitHub vs. Forgejo for a fully-public repo.
**Trade-offs:** A GitHub account is required to contribute via PR (no anonymous push). Mitigated by accepting patches via email or via mirror, both of which the maintainer commits to honoring. Microsoft's AI training on public code is a real concern, but the CC BY-SA license already grants derivative-work rights broadly — model training is one such derivative.
**Revisit if:** GitHub restricts public-repo visibility, introduces a paywall on PR review, or materially degrades the contributor experience for anonymous-or-low-friction contribution; OR Microsoft's posture on public-code AI training changes in a way that undermines the doctrine of forking (e.g., output filters that prevent verbatim doctrine reproduction); OR a Circuit member maintains a Forgejo mirror sufficient to serve as canonical fallback.

## 2026-05-26 — DECISION-AP-DEFER — ActivityPub federation deferred until the practice has scale (per IA §7, §8)
**Chosen:** ActivityPub federation is not built in Phase 4. The site already federates passively via RSS (the Wheel and amendments feeds satisfy IA §7's federation-friendly commitment). ActivityPub remains a "when it serves the practice" eventuality, not a near-term deliverable. The trigger condition for resuming this work is multi-Circuit scale (see Revisit if).
**Alternatives:** (A) Build a minimal ActivityPub actor in Phase 4 — posts amendments to a `@technodruid@technodruid.org` actor. Cost: AP-spec implementation, signed-HTTP-message machinery, follower-state persistence, queue infrastructure, content-moderation surface for follower interactions. (B) Federate via a third-party AP bridge (e.g., Bridgy Fed) — sheds implementation cost but adds vendor dependency and exposes the doctrine to a bridge operator's policy changes. (C) Defer indefinitely without a trigger condition — loses the IA §7 commitment as a real promise.
**Why:** IA §8 line 270 explicitly defers AP "until the practice has scale," and IA §7 line 235 conditions AP on whether it "serves the practice." Scale-before-federation is the doctrinal sequence: a network with no nodes is not federating anything. RSS already covers the federation-friendly commitment at zero operational cost — every Wheel event and every amendment is fetchable by any feed reader, by any indexer, by any Mastodon RSS-bridge a Circuit chooses to operate. ActivityPub's value emerges when there are practitioners reading practitioners across instances; the current network is one site and zero registered Circuits. Building the federation infrastructure now would be infrastructure-first doctrine, which IA §1 rejects.
**Trade-offs:** Without ActivityPub, the site cannot be followed natively from Mastodon/Pleroma/Misskey — Fediverse readers must subscribe via RSS or via a self-hosted AP bridge. This is a real friction for Fediverse-native practitioners. Mitigated by the explicit Revisit trigger: when scale exists, the cost-benefit flips and AP becomes worth building.
**Revisit if:** At least three Circuits are registered AND at least one Circuit hosts a Fediverse presence that would meaningfully interlink with a Technodruid actor; OR a contributor offers to implement and maintain the AP actor at no operational cost to the keeper; OR RSS-only federation becomes inadequate for the amendments ledger (e.g., readers report missed amendments because their feed reader's polling cadence drops them).
